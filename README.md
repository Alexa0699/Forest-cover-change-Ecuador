1. Objective

To analyze changes in forest cover within protected areas in Ecuador during the period 2018–2023.

2. Data Source

The dataset was obtained from the Ministerio del Ambiente of Ecuador in CSV format.
It includes land cover classifications and annual area values for protected areas at the national level.

3. Methods

-Identification and download of the dataset
-Cleaning and preparation of the CSV file
-Transformation of data from wide to long format
-Filtering and aggregation of natural land cover categories
-Visualization of trends using R
-Generation of a complementary QGIS map to visualize the spatial distribution of protected areas

4. Key Findings

Natural forestvshow a decline of approximately 2.26 millions hectares between 2018 and 2023.
Non forest natural formation also presents a decreasing trend during the same period.
Water surface area exhibits an increase, particularly in 2022, suggesting potential land cover transformation dynamics within protected areas.
These results indicate small and heterogeneous trends among natural land cover categories rather than uniform change.

5. Limitations

This analysis does not incorporate other land use categories such as agriculture or urban areas, which may influence overall land cover dynamics.
Additionally, the study focuses exclusively on protected areas and does not include data from the entire national territory.
Further research should investigate the drivers behind the observed increase in water surface area.
